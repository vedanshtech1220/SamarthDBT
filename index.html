Aadhaar‑linked ≠ DBT‑enabled

What students assume

“My account is Aadhaar‑linked, so I will get scholarship funds.”

What is required

Your account must be DBT‑enabled in the NPCI mapper. If not, funds bounce and disbursement is delayed.

Corrective Workflow

  1. System checks NPCI mapper and detects non‑DBT status.
  2. Auto‑generates bank‑specific, pre‑filled consent/mandate form.
  3. Guides you to nearest branch with step‑by‑step instructions.
  4. Sends reminders until activation is confirmed.

FAQs

Is Aadhaar‑linked the same as DBT‑enabled?

No. Aadhaar‑linked means your Aadhaar is connected to your bank account. DBT‑enabled means your account is marked in the NPCI mapper to receive government transfers.

Why did my scholarship bounce?

Most failures happen when the account is not DBT‑enabled in the NPCI mapper, even if Aadhaar is linked.

Is my data safe?

Only essential data is processed; Aadhaar is masked to last 4 digits. All communications use encryption in production.
